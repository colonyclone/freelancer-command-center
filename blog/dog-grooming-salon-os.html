Why Generic Planners Fail Dog Groomers

Notion has thousands of templates. Appointment books, CRMs, inventory trackers—you name it. But none of them understand the specific reality of a grooming business. Here is what makes your workflow different:

  • Two-layer client relationships: You are not just managing the pet owner. You are managing the dog—its breed, coat type, temperament, medical history, vaccination status, and behavioral quirks. A generic CRM has no field for "afraid of dryers" or "needs muzzle for nail trims."
  • Variable pricing by size and service: A Chihuahua bath-and-brush takes 30 minutes. A Standard Poodle full groom takes two hours. Your pricing needs to reflect size, coat condition, and service complexity—not a flat rate.
  • Inventory that directly impacts service: Running out of hypoallergenic shampoo mid-appointment is not an inconvenience. It is a business failure. You need real-time stock tracking with reorder alerts.
  • Recurring revenue through rebooking: Most dogs need grooming every 4–8 weeks. If you are not systematically tracking when each pet is due back, you are leaving money on the table.
Key Insight: The difference between a grooming business that survives and one that thrives is almost always operational—not creative. The best groomers are not always the most profitable. The most organized ones are.

Inside the Dog Grooming Salon OS

This Notion workspace is built around six interconnected databases that eliminate duplicate data entry and give you a complete picture of your business at a glance.

1. The Client Database (Pet Owners)

Store every detail about the humans who bring you their dogs: contact information, preferred groomer, referral source, and lifetime value. The lifetime value field is a rollup that automatically calculates total revenue from each client, so you instantly know who your VIPs are and where your referrals are coming from.

2. The Pet Profiles Database

This is where the magic happens. Each dog gets a detailed profile including breed, size category, coat type, temperament tags (calm, anxious, aggressive, energetic), medical notes, vaccination status, preferred grooming style, and special handling instructions. When a new groomer joins your team, they can look up any dog and know exactly how to handle them before the pet even walks through the door.

3. The Appointments Database

Your operational command center. Every appointment links to both the pet and the owner. Track services rendered, groomer assigned, duration, price, payment method, tips received, and session notes. Use the status tags (Scheduled → Confirmed → In Progress → Completed) to visualize your day. The "Next Appointment" field lets you set recommended rebook dates, turning one-time visitors into loyal regulars.

4. Services & Pricing Database

Define your service menu with base prices, size multipliers, and estimated durations. When you need to adjust pricing for the new year, you update it once here and it cascades through your entire operation.

5. Inventory Management

Track every bottle of shampoo, every clipper blade, every bag of treats. Set reorder points so you get alerted before you run out. Know exactly what your cost-per-groom is by tracking unit costs across all your supplies.

6. Revenue & Expenses Dashboard

Link income directly to appointments and track all business expenses in one place. See your monthly profit at a glance, categorize spending, and have clean financial data ready for tax season.
