What's Inside the Lawn Care & Landscaping Business OS

This isn't a generic "business planner" repackaged for lawn care. Every database, property, and view was designed around the actual workflow of a lawn care professional.

1. Client CRM

Store every client's contact info, source (referral, Google, flyer), lifetime value, and — critically — the notes that keep you professional: gate codes, dog warnings, preferred service days, and payment preferences. When a new crew member joins, they have everything they need without calling you.

2. Property Database

Many clients have multiple properties (rentals, vacation homes, commercial sites). Link each property to its owner, tag the grass type and lot size, and attach photos. When you arrive at a property for the first time, you already know where the side gate is and whether there are sprinkler heads to avoid.

3. Job Scheduler

Schedule one-time or recurring services. Assign crew members. Track quoted vs. actual price. Mark jobs complete with before/after photos. Filter by today's route, this week's schedule, or outstanding invoices. The "Today's Route" dashboard view shows all jobs for the day sorted by address — perfect for your morning coffee review.

4. Equipment & Maintenance Tracker

Log every mower, trimmer, blower, and trailer. Track purchase dates, serial numbers, and service history. Set maintenance reminders so you never blow a $2,000 engine because you skipped an oil change. See the total cost of ownership for each piece of equipment when it's time to upgrade.

5. Revenue & Invoicing

Link invoices to jobs and clients. Track payment status (Draft → Sent → Paid → Overdue). Filter revenue by month, service type, or client. Know exactly how much you've earned from commercial accounts vs. residential, from mowing vs. aeration, without opening a spreadsheet.

6. Seasonal Planning Calendar

Lawn care is seasonal. Plan your spring aeration campaign, summer hedge trimming push, fall leaf cleanup blitz, and winter snow removal prep — all in one calendar view. Attach marketing actions (email blasts, flyer drops) to each seasonal push so you're always generating leads at the right time.
