2. Best Notion Project Tracker for Freelancers

A good project tracker needs three things: visibility into what's due when, a way to see all work for a client at once, and a kanban board you'll actually use. Here's what to look for:

Kanban board by status — You need to be able to see all your projects as cards and move them through stages (Not Started → In Progress → Review → Complete). This is faster than scanning a table.

Linked to clients — A standalone project tracker is half useful. When a client emails about their project, you want to click their name and see everything instantly.

Due date visibility — Ideally with a formula that flags overdue projects automatically. Notion formulas can calculate "days until due" and show a warning if it's negative.

The Freelancer's Command Center includes a project tracker built to all three standards, with a Kanban view, calendar view, and overdue flag formula included.

3. Best Notion Invoice Tracker for Freelancers

Most freelancers lose track of money at some point. A client pays late and you forget to follow up. You invoice someone and then can't remember if they paid. This is fixable with a simple invoice tracking database.

A Notion invoice tracker won't send invoices (use Wave, FreshBooks, or PayPal for that) — but it will tell you exactly what's outstanding, what's paid, and what's overdue at a glance.

Key properties to have in your invoice tracker:

  • Invoice number
  • Client (linked to your client database)
  • Amount + payment status (Draft / Sent / Paid / Overdue)
  • Issue date + due date
  • Payment date (fill in when paid)

With those five things linked, you can filter to "Status = Overdue" and immediately see every client who owes you money, sorted by how long they've been overdue.

FAQ

Do I need a paid Notion plan to use these templates?

No. All templates in this guide work on Notion's free plan. Notion is free at notion.so.

How do Notion templates work — how do I get them into my account?

When you buy a Notion template, you receive a link to a Notion page. Click the link, then click "Duplicate" in the top right corner. Notion copies the entire template — all pages, databases, and data — into your workspace. It takes about 2 minutes.

Can I customize a template after I buy it?

Yes, completely. Once duplicated, the template is yours to edit, rename, delete, or restructure however you want. The original template isn't affected.

What's the difference between a Notion template and a SaaS CRM?

A SaaS CRM (like HoneyBook at $19/month or Dubsado at $20/month) sends automated emails, invoices, and contracts for you. A Notion template doesn't do that — but it costs a fraction of the price and you own it forever. Most freelancers starting out don't need automation; they need clarity. Notion gives you that.

Which template should I start with?

If you're just getting started: the Freelancer's Command Center covers the most ground in one place. If you're already organized but losing clients in your pipeline: the Freelance Sales Pipeline. If you want to look more professional to clients: the Client Welcome Kit.
